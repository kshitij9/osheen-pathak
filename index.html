The Manifesto

The campaigns people remember aren’t remembered because they were the most complicated. They’re remembered because they took something universally true and expressed it in the simplest way possible.

That’s how I think about creative.

I started as a copywriter. Then spent four years editing video, which is where I actually learned that a story isn’t finished when it’s written. It’s rewritten in the edit. Leading post-production taught me how ideas survive execution, or don’t. Creative Direction is where it all came together: strategy, storytelling, craft, production, all in one place.

Having worked every stage means I hold the whole thing in my head at once. I’m thinking about production while I’m still shaping the idea. I’m editing before the camera even rolls. Writing, filmmaking, execution — to me these were never separate disciplines. They’re just different points where the same idea can get protected, or lost.

I’m not chasing beautiful work. I’m interested in making work that people remember years later because it became part of the way they think.
